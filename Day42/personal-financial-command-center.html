Quick tips for students

Track before you budgetLog two weeks of real spending before setting caps — guessed budgets rarely stick.
Use the 24-hour ruleFor non-essential buys over ₹500, wait a day. Impulse spend drops fast.
Automate a small saveEven ₹100/week adds up to ₹5,200/year without you noticing it's gone.
Watch subscription creepReview recurring charges monthly — free trials are the easiest budget leak.

Useful resources

  • Your college's financial aid / bursar office — often has free budgeting workshops
  • A simple notebook ledger as backup — apps fail, habits shouldn't
  • Campus student union — many offer discounted meal plans and transit passes
  • A trusted adult mentor for questions about credit, once you're ready

AI prompts to build financial literacy

  • "Explain compound interest to me like I'm budgeting my allowance."
  • "What's a realistic weekly food budget for a student in my city?"
  • "Help me compare saving vs. spending on [item] with a simple pros/cons list."
  • "Quiz me on 5 basic money terms I should know before I get my first job."
